Questions

Frequently asked.

Is Aether Radio really free?

Yes. Aether Radio is completely free and open source under the MIT license. There are no ads, no subscriptions, no accounts, and no trackers. If you'd like to support development, you can donate via Liberapay.

Where do the radio stations come from?

We use the wonderful community-driven radio-browser.info database. Over 50,000 stations curated by volunteers worldwide. You can also add your own custom streams from any URL.

Which platforms are supported?

Android 6.0 and newer, macOS (Intel and Apple Silicon), Windows 10/11 (x64), Linux via AppImage or .deb, and the web app in any modern browser. iOS is on the roadmap.

Is there a browser version I can use without installing?

Yes — Aether Radio Live runs entirely in your browser with the same 50,000+ stations, song recognition, favorites, sleep timer and 12 accent themes. Two Netlify Edge Functions handle stream CORS and the Shazam signature proxy, so audio plays on any Icecast/Shoutcast source. Your favorites and history stay in localStorage — no account, no cookies, no tracking.

Does song recognition work offline?

Audio fingerprinting happens on-device, but matching against the song database requires an internet connection.

Can I contribute?

Absolutely. Open an issue, send a pull request, or suggest a feature on our GitHub repo. Every bit helps.

What data does Aether Radio collect?

None. Favorites, history and settings live only on your device. The app fetches station metadata from radio-browser mirrors and streams audio directly from each station's server.

Support Aether Radio

Aether Radio is free, open source, and built with love. No ads, no subscriptions, no data collection — ever. If you enjoy the app and want to help keep it alive, consider a small donation. Every contribution goes directly toward development, hosting, and keeping the project independent.
